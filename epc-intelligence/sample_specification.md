# Project Specification: Structural Steel Supply
**Project:** Riverside Commercial Complex — Block A
**Spec Section:** 05 12 00 — Structural Steel Framing
**Revision:** Rev 2.1
**Date:** 2026-01-15
**Issuing Authority:** Lead Structural Engineer

---

## 1. SCOPE

This specification covers the supply, fabrication, and delivery of structural steel members for the primary framing system of Riverside Commercial Complex, Block A. All materials, fabrication standards, and testing requirements defined herein are mandatory unless explicitly waived in writing by the Engineer of Record.

---

## 2. APPLICABLE STANDARDS

The following standards shall govern all work under this section:

- **ASTM A992/A992M** — Standard Specification for Structural Steel Shapes (W-shapes)
- **ASTM A572 Grade 50** — High-Strength Low-Alloy Columbium-Vanadium Structural Steel
- **ASTM A36** — Carbon Structural Steel (secondary members only)
- **AWS D1.1/D1.1M** — Structural Welding Code – Steel
- **AISC 360-22** — Specification for Structural Steel Buildings
- **AISC 303-22** — Code of Standard Practice for Steel Buildings and Bridges

---

## 3. MATERIAL REQUIREMENTS

### 3.1 Primary Steel Members (Columns, Beams, Girders)
- **Grade:** ASTM A992/A992M
- **Minimum Yield Strength:** 50 ksi (345 MPa)
- **Maximum Yield-to-Tensile Ratio:** 0.85
- **Maximum Carbon Equivalent:** 0.45%
- **Mill Certification:** Required for every heat number. Certified Mill Test Reports (CMTRs) must accompany each shipment.

### 3.2 Secondary Members (Purlins, Bracing, Girts)
- **Grade:** ASTM A36 or ASTM A572 Grade 50
- **Minimum Yield Strength:** 36 ksi (248 MPa)

### 3.3 Bolts and Fasteners
- **Structural Bolts:** ASTM F3125 Grade A325 or A490
- **Anchor Rods:** ASTM F1554 Grade 55 minimum
- **All bolts shall be galvanized** per ASTM A153 for exterior and exposed locations.

### 3.4 Prohibited Materials
- Recycled or remelted steel from unknown origin is strictly prohibited.
- Steel with mill certifications older than 18 months shall not be used without re-testing.

---

## 4. FABRICATION REQUIREMENTS

### 4.1 Shop Drawings
- Contractor shall submit shop drawings for Engineer review a **minimum of 21 calendar days** prior to fabrication commencement.
- Shop drawings shall include: member sizes, connection details, weld symbols, bolt patterns, camber requirements, and erection marks.

### 4.2 Tolerances
- Member length tolerance: ±3 mm for members ≤ 9 m; ±5 mm for members > 9 m
- Column plumb tolerance: H/1000 or 25 mm maximum, whichever is less
- Beam camber tolerance: ±12 mm from specified camber

### 4.3 Welding
- All welding shall be performed by **AWS D1.1 certified welders** only.
- Prequalified weld procedures (WPS) must be submitted prior to fabrication.
- Full Penetration Welds (CJP) at all primary moment connections.
- **Non-Destructive Testing (NDT):** 100% UT for CJP welds; 25% MT for fillet welds.

### 4.4 Surface Preparation and Coating
- All steel surfaces shall be blast cleaned to **SSPC-SP 6 (Commercial Blast)** minimum before priming.
- **Primer:** Zinc-rich epoxy primer, minimum 75 microns DFT.
- **Finish coat** (exposed areas): High-build epoxy, minimum 125 microns DFT.
- Touch-up of field welds and damaged areas is mandatory before erection is complete.

---

## 5. TESTING AND INSPECTION

### 5.1 Mill Testing
- CMTRs shall be provided for **100% of heats** used in primary members.
- Independent third-party verification of CMTRs is required for orders exceeding 50 tonnes.

### 5.2 Dimensional Inspection
- 100% dimensional inspection of all primary members prior to shipment.
- Inspection reports shall be submitted to the Engineer within **5 business days** of completion.

### 5.3 Coating Inspection
- Dry Film Thickness (DFT) to be measured per **SSPC-PA 1** using calibrated gauges.
- Minimum 5 readings per 10 m² of surface area.

### 5.4 Rejection Criteria
- Any member failing dimensional, material, or coating requirements shall be clearly tagged and segregated.
- Rejected members shall not be shipped without written Engineer approval.

---

## 6. DELIVERY AND DOCUMENTATION

### 6.1 Delivery Schedule
- Fabricator shall provide a **detailed delivery schedule** 14 days prior to first shipment.
- Partial deliveries permitted only with Engineer pre-approval.

### 6.2 Required Submittals (to be provided before or with first shipment)
1. Certified Mill Test Reports (CMTRs) for all heats
2. Shop Drawing approval set (stamped by Engineer)
3. Weld Procedure Specifications (WPS) and Welder Qualification Records (WQR)
4. Coating system data sheets and application records
5. Dimensional inspection reports
6. Third-party NDT reports

### 6.3 Marking
- All members shall be clearly marked with erection marks matching shop drawings.
- Heat numbers shall be traceable to CMTRs.

---

## 7. WARRANTY

- Fabricator warrants all materials and workmanship for a period of **24 months** from substantial completion.
- Warranty covers dimensional non-conformances, material failures, and coating delamination attributable to fabrication defects.
